Descriptive Analytics

Descriptive analytics helps us understand what has happened in the past regarding customer churn. By analyzing historical data, we can identify patterns and trends that provide insights into the factors influencing customer decisions to leave.

This chart displays the distribution of monthly charges for customers, segmented by churn status. It shows that customers who churn (in blue) are more frequent in the mid-to-high monthly charge ranges, while those who stay (in red) dominate the lower charge brackets. Notably, a large number of loyal customers are concentrated at the lowest monthly charges, suggesting that lower pricing may contribute to customer retention.

This chart illustrates the distribution of customer tenure (in months) by churn status. It shows that customers with very short tenure—around 0 to 5 months—have a high churn rate (shown in blue), indicating early dissatisfaction. In contrast, customers with longer tenure, especially those beyond 60 months, are less likely to churn and are more loyal (shown in red). This suggests that customer retention improves significantly as tenure increases.

This chart visualizes the relationship between Customer Lifetime Value (CLTV), Monthly Charges, and Churn. It shows that customers with higher monthly charges are slightly more likely to churn, regardless of their CLTV. In contrast, customers with lower monthly charges tend to stay. While CLTV alone doesn't show a strong link to churn, higher monthly costs appear to increase the likelihood of a customer leaving.
