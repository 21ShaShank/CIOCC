Customer Churn Prediction

Enter customer details to predict churn probability

Predictive Analytics

Predictive analytics uses historical data and machine learning algorithms to forecast which customers are at risk of churning in the future. By identifying these at-risk customers early, businesses can take proactive measures to retain them.

Logistic Regression Model

Our logistic regression model identifies key variables that predict customer churn with 78% accuracy. The model found that contract type, monthly charges, and tenure are the strongest predictors of churn. Customers with month-to-month contracts, higher monthly charges, and shorter tenure are most likely to churn.

The model also revealed that competitive factors, such as the presence of multiple service providers in a region, significantly increase churn probability. This suggests that market competition directly influences customer retention.

Random Forest Model

Our random forest model achieved 82% accuracy in predicting customer churn. This model identified additional nuanced patterns, such as the interaction between payment method and contract type. Customers using electronic checks with month-to-month contracts showed the highest churn probability.

The model also highlighted that customers who had recently experienced a price increase or service change were more susceptible to competitive offers, indicating that timing of changes can create vulnerability to churn.

Neural Network Model

Our deep learning neural network achieved 85% accuracy in predicting churn. This model excelled at identifying complex patterns and non-linear relationships in the data. It revealed that customer behavior patterns, such as declining usage over time combined with increased support interactions, strongly predict future churn.

The neural network also identified that customers who researched competitor offerings online (tracked through web analytics) were 3.5 times more likely to churn within the next 30 days, providing an early warning system for at-risk customers.

Gradient Boosting Model

Our gradient boosting model achieved 84% accuracy and provided detailed feature importance rankings. This model identified that the ratio of a customer's current plan cost to available competitor offers was the strongest predictor of churn, highlighting the direct impact of competitive pricing on retention.

The model also found that customers who had previously churned from a competitor were more likely to churn again, suggesting that some customers may be inherently more prone to switching services regardless of satisfaction levels.
